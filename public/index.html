enterprise tier guarantees (v0.6.0)

deterministic detection, gated by a fixture corpus in CI. if any promised tier ever drops below 100%, the deploy fails — pre-deploy offline and post-deploy against this live host.

  • L1 vendor secrets — 100%. openai, anthropic, github pat (classic + fine-grained), stripe, slack, aws, google, hf, supabase, notion, linear, twilio, sendgrid, mailgun, postmark, shopify, square, heroku, digitalocean, npm, pypi, docker, gitlab, jwt, pem blocks.
  • L2 structured ids — 100%. credit cards (luhn-validated), ssn, ipv4.
  • L3 env-var sweep — 100%. NAME_KEY|TOKEN|SECRET|PASSWORD|... = value, including quoted values with spaces.
  • L4 contact — 100%. email and urls carrying token/key/secret/sig/auth/session params.
  • L5 free-form pii — best-effort. phone numbers and prose-only identifiers. add a rule pack or post-process for the cases your domain requires.

what it isn't

  • not a free-form pii guarantee. L1–L4 are 100%-promised and ci-gated; L5 (novel names, context-dependent pii) requires ner and is best-effort. add a rule pack or post-process if you need it.
  • reversible by design. mappings are stored encrypted under an env key — that's a *feature* for audit and legal-hold, not a bug. document it honestly to your buyers.
  • symmetric hs256 key management today. v0.5 added kid rotation with env-managed keys; kms-backed key management is on the roadmap.
